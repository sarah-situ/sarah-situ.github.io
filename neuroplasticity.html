Neuroplasticity & Mindset

### • Discuss how understanding the principles of neuroplasticity benefits people. Neuroplasticity is the process where the brain forms new neurons and create new pathways with these neurons. In response to learning, these new neural pathways are the brain’s way of changing and organising new information to build further connections. While at a younger age we tend to be more responsive to learning and this is the period where the brain has a rapid ground in neurons and there are around 15,000 connections(synapses)(2) per neurons in the brains of an average three year old. As an average adult we have half of these numbers, because as we age we experience something called synaptic pruning. This is because we gain new experiences, we build and strengthen connections and some are removed. However neuroplasticity is an ongoing process and everyone can benefit from it. Brain development does not stop after early childhood, but it is the foundation upon which the brain continues developing. (3). The brain is adaptable and continues to change throughout our lives. The more we practice something, the brain continues to strengthen and build new pathways for a new habit or if you are learning a new language. **10 Principles of Neuroplasticity: Part 1** [https://www.cfimove.org/10-principles-of-neuroplasticity-part-1/#:~:text=It is also the mechanism,to repeat the learned behavior](https://www.cfimove.org/10-principles-of-neuroplasticity-part-1/#:~:text=It%20is%20also%20the%20mechanism,to%20repeat%20the%20learned%20behavior). https://www.verywellmind.com/what-is-brain-plasticity-2794886 (2)https://extension.umaine.edu/publications/4356e/ (3) https://extension.umaine.edu/publications/4356e/ ## • Discuss how you might engage with the principles of neuroplasticity for your benefit. https://accelerate.uofuhealth.utah.edu/resilience/neuroplasticity-how-to-use-your-brain-s-malleability-to-improve-your-well-being Knowing that fact that you can change your brain, you can learning something new with some repetition and patience is helpful.
