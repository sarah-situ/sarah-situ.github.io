Emotional Intelligence

What is Emotional Intelligence?

Emotional Intelligence is the ability to manage your own emotions and also understand the emotions of the people around you.

There are five key elements to Emotional Intelligence:

Self-awareness

Being able to identify how you are feeling, how those feelings affect to yourself, the situation and others. Being aware helps you to identify why you or someone is behaving in a certain way. Self awareness is also knowing your strengths and weaknesses.

Self-regulation

To be able to self-regulate allows you to avoid making impulsive decisions. You are able to think more clearly and objectively compared to if you make you a rash decision based on your feelings at the time.

Motivation and Empathy

Understanding others with empathy and understanding is a crucial part of teamwork. Being empathetic is important to building relationships and making others feel heard. Empathy can motivate prosocial behaviour( actions that improve the lives of others). People with high emotional intelligence are motivated by internal rewards(goals), action-orientated and are always searching ways to improve.

Social Skills

To be able to interact well with others is an important emotional intelligence skill. These include active listening, verbal and nonverbal communication skills that allow you to build meaningful relationships and interactions.


How is EQ different to IQ?

EQ (Emotional quotient) is the ability to understand emotions in yourself and in other people.
It is also the ability to use that knowledge to empathise and communicate well with others.

IQ (Intelligence quotient) relates to your cognitive and intellectual skills, using logic to solve problems, to learn and grasps new information and ideas.


Why is EQ important?

Having EQ is important because it helps you to build strong relationships, personal and professional. To be able to understand the behaviour of others can give insight to their emotions and needs, this information can allow us to empathise and communicate better with others. It also helps you to be in tuned with yourself and encourage us to set goals and make conscious decisions that are good for our well-being.
